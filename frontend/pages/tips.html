Tips & Tricks

Ace your next interview with these proven strategies

Technical Interviews
Think out loud. Interviewers want to follow your reasoning, not just see the answer. Narrate every step.
Clarify before coding. Ask about edge cases, input constraints, and expected output before writing a single line.
Start with brute force. A working O(n²) solution is better than no solution. Then optimize.
Test your code. Walk through examples manually before saying you're done.
Behavioral Interviews
Use the STAR method. Situation → Task → Action → Result. Every behavioral answer should follow this structure.
Be specific. Avoid vague answers. Use real numbers, timelines, and measurable outcomes.
Prepare 5–7 stories. Cover: leadership, conflict, failure, success, teamwork, innovation, and learning.
Show growth. When discussing failures, emphasize what you learned and what you'd do differently.
System Design
Start with requirements. Ask about scale (users, requests/sec), latency needs, and consistency requirements.
Draw the high-level design first. Clients → Load Balancer → Services → DB. Expand from there.
Talk about trade-offs. SQL vs NoSQL, consistency vs availability, monolith vs microservices.
Address bottlenecks. Where will the system fail under load? Caching, sharding, CDNs.
Voice Interview Tips
Speak at a natural pace. Rushing causes filler words. Take a breath before answering.
Structure your answer first. Pause, think, then speak. 5 seconds of silence is better than rambling.
Eliminate filler words. Replace "um", "uh", "like" with a pause. The AI scores you on this.
Aim for 2–3 minutes. Long enough to be thorough, short enough to stay focused.
